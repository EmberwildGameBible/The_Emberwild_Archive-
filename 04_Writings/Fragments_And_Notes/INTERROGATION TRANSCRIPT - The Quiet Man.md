# INTERROGATION TRANSCRIPT — “The Quiet Man”

*Recovered from a Heartland barracks. The suspect’s handwriting and voice analysis don’t match any known record.*

**Officer:** Name?

**Suspect:** Doesn’t matter.

**Officer:** It does. Write it here.

**Suspect:** That’s not mine. I don’t use that name anymore.

**Officer:** What happened to the family in the cottage?

**Suspect:** I don’t know them.

**Officer:** Witness says they saw you carrying tools toward their fence.

**Suspect:** Tools aren’t illegal.

**Officer:** Fence was cut. Dog was gone.

**Suspect:** Dog ran. Happens.

**Officer:** There were footprints.

**Suspect:** You’re wasting time. I didn’t touch them.

**Officer:** So why run when patrol called out?

**Suspect:** Habit.

*(Long pause. Scratching sounds.)*

**Officer:** Tell me why you were there.

**Suspect:** I was looking for someone else’s house.

**Officer:** Who?

**Suspect:** Doesn’t matter now.

**Officer:** Why them?

**Suspect:** They know why.

**Officer:** Who are you really?

**Suspect:** I’m the one who fixes things people pretend not to see.

*(Interrogation ends early. Suspect escaped custody that night. No signs of forced exit.)*
