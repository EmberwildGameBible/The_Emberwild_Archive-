# **PAGE I —**

# **“THE LEDGER OF WEIGHT”**

(Pre-Sundering, written by an Old World record-keeper)

We were told to count grain, coin, bodies, borders.

No one asked us to count what the world felt.

**In-Game Page Text (what the player reads)**

[ENTRY I]

I begin this ledger under protest. A weight cannot be measured in stone alone, nor grain, nor bodies stacked. Yet the Ministers insist:

“Record not only what is taken, but what it costs.”

So I have walked the districts and kept what figures I could:

- One man worked to blood for a loaf.
- Ten sent to quarry rock who will not return.
- A village’s well bricked over to divert water to an estate.
- A child whipped for taking what was already hers.

These are not numbers, they are angles—all leaning the same way.

They have no column in the books, so I make one. I name it Burden.

For each act, I give it weight by what it bends: backs, laws, oaths, sleep.

We are past counting coin. We are counting tilt.

[ENTRY II]

The Ministers feared revolt, not collapse. They feared torches, not fault-lines.

I warned them:

“When Burden grows faster than Rest, the stone takes note.”

They laughed and asked me where the stone keeps its ledger.

I did not have the words then, so I write them now:

The land remembers the sum of what is asked of it.

Every lash that draws blood, every hunger that gnaws through bone,

every oath broken for comfort alone—

these press downward.

Every mercy given where none was owed, every hand that lifts without demand,

every quiet kindness done at cost—

these press back.

We are no longer near balance. I scratch tallies until the ink cuts the page.

The columns lean all one way.

[ENTRY III]

I have begun to mark not only acts, but the places where they are worst.

There are districts where the air itself feels bent,

where sound dies too quickly and the ground gives a little underfoot.

In my private mark I call them Riven Places.

When a hanging is cheered;

when a guest is starved to feed a lord’s hounds;

when a worker is sealed into the wall he built to save time—

I note not only the deed, but where it was done.

The pattern is clear enough now that I need no further proof:

Burden gathers like silt in hollows,

and the hollows deepen to hold it.

If we do not turn the sum, the land will turn itself.

Not by petition, nor revolt, but by giving way.

Stone will choose its weakest lines.

When that day comes, let this ledger stand witness:

the world did not break by accident.

It was weighed down until it slipped.

That’s the player-facing text.

Now here’s how the hidden puzzle layers work on this page.

**Developer Notes (Ciphers & Hooks)**

(Not shown to players)

Give this page a subtle formatting trick:

So:

- This points them toward a shop / general goods store with a private ledger stored in the back.
- That ledger can be where Page II is hidden (behind shelf, crate, etc.).

You don’t need to hard-code the exact shop name into this page; the acrostic just suggests what kind of place and what to check.

This ratio can:

- or correspond to a location index (e.g., 7th page, 3rd line).

You don’t have to expose this yet; Page I just needs to establish the idea that the world has a measurable imbalance.

**3. Deep Meta Hook – “Riven Places”**

The phrase Riven Places in ENTRY III is your forward-hook into the Riven Undermarch & First Wound.

You can secretly tag:

- any region you want as RivenPlace = true in your world data.
- later, when another page references Riven Places, you’ll have:
    - a consistent lore term,

You don’t have to solve all that now; this page just introduces the concept.

# **PAGE II —**

# **“THE FIRST CALCULUS OF RUIN”**

(Pre-Sundering scientific analysis. Cold, clinical, frightened beneath the surface.)

This Page must feel like the moment the scholars realized the world wasn’t just politically collapsing — it was physically failing.

It follows Page I’s emotional tone but drops deeper into technical dread.

Below is the full in-game text, followed by the cipher layers and how Page II leads players toward Page III.

# **IN-GAME PAGE TEXT**

[ENTRY I — On the Ratios]

We refined the Ledger after the Ministers dismissed it.

The columns were wrong. The categories insufficient.

“Burden” was too broad; “Rest,” too small.

So we divided Burden into its truer parts:

- Imposed Weight — the harm done by command.
- Inherited Weight — the harm done by lineage.
- Desperate Weight — the harm done by need.
- Silent Weight — the harm done by turning away.

These values vary by district, by season, by decree.

Yet their sum rises without interruption.

We compared them to the Rest measures—meager, faint—and saw the truth:

the ratios no longer drift.

They tilt.

They gather into a slope no law can stand upon.

[ENTRY II — On the First Signs]

The stone speaks sooner than the people.

We began to measure hairline fractures in the lower quarries.

First by nail, then by paper-width, then by the width of a child’s finger.

The mason-masters called it settling.

But stone settles down; these fractures pulled sideways,

as though the earth itself shifted its shoulders.

These cracks mapped perfectly to the districts

with the highest concentrations of Imposed and Silent Weight.

Our world bends beneath deeds as surely as beams bend beneath load.

[ENTRY III — On the Projection]

The calculus is simple now.

If Burden continues its rise at this pace,

then Rest cannot counterbalance it by law or mercy.

The tilt becomes irreversible.

The land will choose the path of least resistance.

There is a line—drawn not on any map but in the stone—

where the pressure will give way first.

I cannot publish it.

I cannot even speak it.

I have hidden the coordinates where only those who truly look

will find what the Ministers would burn.

When you see the numbers, you will understand why

the world cannot remain whole.

That is the full player-facing text.

Now here are the hidden mechanisms.

Because Page III is acquired only by fishing during fog.

This is how Page II leads players to the right place without ever saying it directly.

# **PAGE III —**

# **“THE BURDENED CHARTER”**

(Pre-Sundering; written during the last weeks before collapse. Tone is tense, resigned, bureaucratic despair.)

This Page must feel like the scholars realized the government will do nothing, and the world is sliding toward its fault-line.

It bridges the cold analysis of Page II with the panic in Page IV.

Below is the full in-game text, followed by the three cipher layers, and how Page III points players to Page IV.

# **IN-GAME PAGE TEXT**

[ENTRY I — The Ministers’ Reply]

The Charter was read this morning behind closed doors.

We listed the ratios, we mapped the fractures,

we showed the tilt in clear ink on clean pages.

The Ministers nodded and said the sums were troubling.

Then they asked whether the figures could be “softened,”

since unrest was rumored in the western districts.

They do not want truth—

they want shape.

Something rounder, gentler, less sharp to the eye.

I refused.

They dismissed me for the day.

But the stone does not soften when we lie to it.

[ENTRY II — The District Audit]

I visited three sites marked in the Ledger as Riven Places.

In the first, a well was sunk ten spans deeper than before,

though no one had dug it.

In the second, a grain cellar had new cracks

that crept along the ceiling like veins.

In the third, the air itself felt cold despite the hearths—

the kind of cold that comes from pressure, not weather.

A mason examined the fractures with me.

He placed his hand on the stone and frowned.

“Something is pulling,” he said.

“Not down. Away.”

[ENTRY III — The Quiet Petition]

A few of us have drafted a private petition.

A final plea for mitigation.

Not to the Ministers—they have ears but no listening.

To the builders, the diggers, the riders, the mothers, the elders—

to anyone who still lives with consequence.

We ask them not to rebel,

but to rest where they can

and lift where they are able.

A thousand mercies could buy the land a single breath.

A thousand hands could slow the tilt by a measure.

But we fear it is already late.

If the Charter is ignored, let this page bear record:

the collapse will not begin with thunder.

It will begin with a silence

as the stone finds its line.

# **CIPHER STRUCTURE OF PAGE III**

Page III contains three layered puzzles that point to Page IV in a unique way.

# **LAYER 1 — Alternating-Word Cipher (Leads to the shack)**

In ENTRY I, hide a sentence structure like this:

“The Ministers nodded and said the sums were troubling.

Inside this paragraph, every second word (or every third — choose a rule) forms a message like:

This is the first hint that Page IV is found behind a door that appears unimportant and dead quiet.

This points toward:

# **LAYER 2 — Timestamp Cipher (Weather/Time Condition for Page IV)**

In ENTRY II, hide three numbers disguised as measurements:

- “ten spans deeper”
- “three sites”
- “cold despite the hearths” (cold = temperature)

Extracting the numbers gives: 10 / 3 / (cold) → combine these into:

Day 10, Third Fog Pulse

Or more loosely:

Fog + 3 units after dawn

You can interpret “cold” as fog condition.

This means:

**Page IV can only spawn if the abandoned shack is approached during fog.**

# **LAYER 3 — Audio Cipher (Requires rhythm recognition)**

In ENTRY III, have three lines written with slightly odd rhythm:

“A thousand mercies could buy the land a single breath.

A thousand hands could slow the tilt by a measure.

But we fear it is already late.”

The structure is long — long — short.

This rhythm (long, long, short) corresponds to knock-knock-tap.

So Page III’s deepest clue is:

**Knock twice, then softly once, on the shack door.**

The shack must be scripted to listen for this pattern.

- On return, the Page IV parchment is now inside on a table or wall

# **IN-GAME PAGE TEXT**

Found in a locked, abandoned shack after the knock-pattern.

[ENTRY I — The First Answer]

The Charter went unheard, but the stone did not wait for permission.

It answered in the night.

No wind.

No tremor.

Only a single sound—

a crack so thin and sharp it seemed to split the air before the earth.

The mason with me dropped his tools and whispered,

“That was not settling.”

[ENTRY II — The Choir]

Then came the others.

Not thunder.

Not collapse.

A chorus.

Stone striking stone along a widening line—

taps, knocks, groans, and sharp reports

echoing inward from every Riven Place marked in the Ledger.

Each sound answered the last,

spreading like a thought moving through a crowd.

The earth was speaking.

And it was counting.

[ENTRY III — The Split]

We ran to the ridge above the lower quarry.

By torchlight we saw the seam.

It did not open downward.

It opened apart.

The land peeled itself like rind from rind,

slow, deliberate, almost careful—

but unstoppable.

The torch flame bent sideways.

Our breath fled from us.

I understood then what the Ledger had shown:

the weight had become direction.

When the seam finished its measure,

sections of the quarry floor dropped in sheets,

sliding away from one another as though ashamed to share their burden.

The mason wept.

I wrote until the ink shook from my hands.

The Ministers will call this an accident.

But we have heard the stone choose its line.

This is the first page showing active catastrophe.

(Early-Sundering; written by one stationed near the Tree during the first true rupture. Tone: fearful, reverent, intimate. The first Page directly observing the Tree’s reaction.)

# **IN-GAME PAGE TEXT**

Found on the corpse in the ravine — the Rootwatcher died mid-observation.

[ENTRY I — The First Pulse]

I felt the first pulse just past dusk.

The ground beneath the roots did not tremble—

it drew breath.

A slow inhale that lifted dust from the stones

and stirred the lantern flame against its wick.

The Tree did not sway.

The branches did not move.

But every hair on my arms rose

as if something behind the bark had opened an eye.

[ENTRY II — The Sound Beneath]

I pressed my ear to the rootline slab.

I know what settling sounds like.

This was not settling.

This was counting.

A low rhythm—four, then three, then four again—

soft as a heartbeat, wrong as a wound remembering its pain.

The pulses traveled outward,

past the ridge,

past the quarry line,

toward the districts we marked as Riven Places.

[ENTRY III — The Marking]

I left my post to follow the pulses.

My orders forbade it,

but each rhythm pulled at me like a hand on my collar.

I crossed the ridge, descended the eastern cut,

and reached the quarry’s splintered edge just as the sound deepened.

The seam widened before me.

No dust.

No roar.

Just the land dividing as quietly as lips parting for a truth too long held.

The pulses quickened.

My lantern dimmed.

I turned back toward the Tree.

From this distance its canopy looked wrong—

not moving, but listening.

I am writing this on the return climb.

If the pulses continue, the land will soon choose its second line.

I must return to warn the others.

If I do not reach them, let this testimony stand:

the Tree is not breaking—

it is bearing witness.

[ENTRY I — The Pressure]

When I reached the ridge, the pulses had stopped.

Not faded—stopped,

as though the world held breath between its teeth.

The seam before me widened without sound.

Dust did not rise.

The lantern flame leaned toward the gap as if drawn by a tide in the air.

I felt a pressure in my jaw, my ears, my spine.

Not like falling—

like being remembered by something that had forgotten me.

[ENTRY II — The Unseen]

Then the cold rolled out.

A cold without wind,

without moisture,

without cause.

Shapes moved at the edge of the seam.

Not bodies—

suggestions of bodies:

bending,

pausing,

turning toward me

as though hearing something behind my heartbeat.

They did not cross.

They only leaned,

like shadows pulled taut toward flame.

[ENTRY III — The Breath]

The gap widened.

The pressure sharpened.

And then—

A sound like cloth tearing underwater.

Not loud.

Not soft.

A sound that felt like it happened inside my ribs.

My lantern burst.

The shapes recoiled.

The cold withdrew with a sucking sigh.

For a moment, the seam glowed as though lit from its far side.

Not bright—

but present.

I write this shaking.

Whatever lay behind that light did not come through,

but it breathed,

and the breath did not belong to our world.

We will not reach the Ministers.

If this page is found—

let it stand that the second measure was taken in good faith.

The first told us when the world would break.

The second tells us where.
