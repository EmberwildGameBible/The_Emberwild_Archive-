# FAMILY 12 — LARGE REPTILES & LIZARDS

“Slow heat. Fast violence.”

These are the cold-blooded heavyweights of Emberwild:

- monitor-sized lizards
- swamp lizards
- desert scalebacks
- ridge monitors
- marshwalkers
- coastal rock-lizards
- tundra ice-lizards (rare but real)

Not dragons.

Not fantasy beasts.

Not magical.

Just real-world grounded reptile analogs scaled up to the limits of biological plausibility.

They occupy a unique ecological space:

slow until provoked, then explosive.

────────────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.12.0 — FAMILY IDENTITY

Large reptiles represent:

- patience
- explosive ambush
- territorial intimidation
- crushing bite pressure
- surprising speed over short distances
- biome-adapted camouflage

They are the mid-tier to high-tier heavy predators of warm and wet biomes.

Not as massive as bears,

not as fast as raptors,

but extremely dangerous in close quarters.

────────────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.12.1 — BIOLOGY & ECOLOGY

### Habitats

Different species thrive in:

- Swamps (marsh monitors, mud-dragons)
- Forests (ridge lizards)
- Deserts (sand monitors, scalebacks)
- Coasts (rockshore lizards)
- Tundra edges (rare ice-scaled lizards)

### Diet

- fish
- carrion
- small mammals
- waterfowl
- eggs
- reptiles
- occasionally deer fawns

### Behavior Type

- Ambush predators
- Bite-and-hold attackers
- Heat-driven aggressors
- Territory guardians

### Season Influence

- Hot weather: maximal mobility and aggression
- Cold weather: extremely slow or hibernating
- Rain: increased stalking in wetlands
- Fog: stealth advantage

# SECTION 30.12.2 — TIER VARIANTS

(Using your 6-tier grounded structure)

### NORMAL LIZARD

- moderate size
- short dash attack
- medium bite damage
- stable behavior; flees often
- common in Outer/Inner zones

### RARE LIZARD

- unique scale coloration
- larger frame
- sharper bite
- better sprint window
- more territorial

### ELITE LIZARD

- big, scarred, powerful reptile
- thick tail swipe (knockdown)
- deadly close-range bite
- longer ambush patience
- used as a “mini-gator” style threat

### RARE ELITE LIZARD

- massive swamp/rock/heat-borne alpha
- deeply ridged scales
- near-perfect camouflage
- terrifying burst speed
- signature drops:
    - Thick Scaleplate
    - Ancient Fang

### MINI-BOSS LIZARD

NOT tamable.

Often called:

- “Mud Tyrant”
- “Rockback Gnasher”
- “Sand Sovereign”

Characteristics:

- handcrafted AI
- double dash → bite
- tail sweep → stagger
- roll-over thrash
- found in deep wetlands, dunes, or old ruins

### BOSS

Dungeon-only:

- subterranean lizard boss
- cave ambush multi-phase fight

# SECTION 30.12.3 — TRACKING SIGNS

Large reptiles leave clear but subtle signs due to low gait and drags.

### Tracks

- dragging belly line
- clawed footprints
- deeper in wet mud
- Rare/Elite show heavier body weight

### Tail Marks

- long side-sweep marks
- often the easiest clue
- curved arcs around ambush sites

### Scale Sheds

- small flakes or plates
- sharp-edged in Rare/Elite

### Mud Slides

- smoothed patches from basking
- churned mud near water

### Kill Signs

- punctured prey
- dragged carcasses
- stripped bones

### Heat Basking Signs

- flattened grasses or brush
- warm rocks with shed marks

────────────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.12.4 — AI BEHAVIOR & COMBAT LOGIC

Large reptiles are ambush → burst → retreat predators.

### Primary Behaviors

- sudden lunge bites
- tail sweep (knockdown)
- open-mouth intimidation
- hold-and-shake

### Ambush Logic

Triggers when:

- the player gets too close
- entering a wet patch or rock alcove
- hearing footsteps near water

### Bite Behavior

- massive puncture
- stagger
- shake damage on Elite+

### Temperature Logic

- high heat = increased aggression
- cold = slow, sluggish, low threat

### Flee Logic

- Normal: 50%
- Rare: 40%
- Elite: 25%
- Rare Elite: 10%
- Mini-Boss: never

────────────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.12.5 — WEATHER INTERACTION

### Rain

- swamp species become hyper-active
- desert species shelter under rocks

### Fog

- huge stealth advantage

### Heatwave

- desert monitors → most dangerous state
- swamp lizards sun themselves and attack anything nearby

### Snow

- near-hibernation
- extremely low chance of attack

────────────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.12.6 — COMBAT PROFILE

### Damage

- heavy bite
- crushing tail strike
- bleed from tooth punctures
- thrash (Elite+)

### Defenses

- medium HP
- high armor on scales
- strong resistance to slashing
- low bleed intake

### Weaknesses

- vulnerable to blunt damage
- slow turning
- predictable after first attack
- poor against fire

# SECTION 30.12.8 — TAMER COMPANION SUMMARY

Large reptiles CAN be companions, but only:

- Normal
- Rare
- Elite

Rare Elite + Mini-Boss cannot be tamed.

### Companion Role: BURST / CONTROL / INTIMIDATION

### Strengths

- strong bite
- good knockdown pressure
- intimidating to other wildlife
- loyal but slow temperament
- excellent in wetlands & deserts

### Weaknesses

- slow turn rate
- low stamina in cold regions
- mediocre morale
- slow bonding process

### Companion Behaviors

- Lunge Bite
- Tail Sweep
- Intimidate
- Hold & Shake (Elite only)
- Ambush Assist

They are the bruiser reptiles of the companion ecosystem.

# SECTION 30.12.9 — FAMILY ROLE IN WORLD DESIGN

Large reptiles complete Emberwild by:

- defining swamp & desert danger
- adding non-mammalian predators
- supporting heavy hide & scale crafting
- providing ambush threats near water
- building biome identity

They finalize the predator triangle:

mammal → bird → reptile, each with unique threat rhythms.
