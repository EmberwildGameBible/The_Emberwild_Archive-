# FAMILY 7 — TURTLES

“They do not hurry. They do not fear. They simply endure.”

Turtles in Emberwild are not monsters.

They are not magical guardians.

They are armored fixtures of the ecosystem — subtle, defensive, territorial, and extremely durable when threatened.

This family spans:

- marsh turtles
- forest tortoises
- highland rockbacks
- coastal shelled grazers
- desert hardbacks

All grounded and biological.

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.0 — FAMILY IDENTITY

Turtles represent:

- extreme durability
- slow, deliberate movement
- territorial defense
- survival by patience
- grounded realism
- nature’s “living bulwark”

They are not predators in most cases — but they are dangerous when provoked, especially large Heartland variants.

They excel at:

- damage soaking
- shrugging off arrows
- pushing enemies
- resisting bleed/poison
- controlling tight terrain

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.1 — BIOLOGY & ECOLOGY

### Habitats

Different species thrive in different biomes:

- Swamps / Wetlands → Softshell Marsh Turtles
- Forests → Lowland Tortoises
- Highlands → Rockback Turtles
- Coasts → Shoreback Grazers
- Desert Edges → Hardscale Tortoises

### Diet

- grasses
- roots
- mushrooms
- low-lying plants
- insects (rare)
- carrion (very rarely)

### Behavior Type

- Defensive Herbivores
- Territorial When Threatened
- Slow Endurance Movers
- Shell Retraction When Wounded

### Season Influence

- Spring: high movement, mating
- Summer: moderate, forage-rich
- Autumn: shell-thickening (hardening seasonal growth)
- Winter: low activity, hibernation for certain breeds

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.2 — TIER VARIANTS

Using Emberwild’s official tier system:

### NORMAL TURTLE

- slow movement
- strong defense
- low damage
- harmless unless provoked
- easy to track (slow pathing)

### RARE TURTLE

- unique shell patterns / colors
- improved shell hardness
- higher stamina
- territorial snaps
- found in Inner zones

### ELITE TURTLE

- very large, old individuals
- deeply scarred shells
- extremely resistant to slashing
- heavy shove attack
- can “brace” for reduced damage

### RARE ELITE TURTLE

- massive, ancient shelled beasts
- thick, rock-like shell ridges
- notorious for pushing predators off cliffs/edges
- signature drop:
    - Ancient Shellplate
    - Tough Tortoise Ridge

### MINI-BOSS TURTLE

NOT tamable.

Often called:

- “Oldstone”
- “The Ridgefather”
- “Shellback Titan”

Characteristics:

- handcrafted AI
- extremely heavy slam + shell-bash
- hunkers down, taking 90% reduced frontal damage
- zone defender in Heartlands rivers or highland passes

### BOSS TURTLE

Dungeon-only:

- found in caverns, mines, or flooded ruins
- phase-based shell retreat + stomp

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.3 — TRACKING SIGNS

Turtle tracking relies on slow but obvious environmental changes.

### Shell Drag Marks

- shallow grooves in dirt or mud
- wide, slow, continuous
- extremely distinctive

### Footprints

- small, blunt, round prints
- Rare/Elite have deeper impressions

### Plant Damage

- grazed mushrooms
- scraped bark at low height
- crushed grass trails

### Droppings

- solid, fibrous
- easy to identify

### Water Evidence (Swamp/Coastal)

- disturbed mud
- sliding tracks into or out of water
- algae smear patterns

Turtle trails are long-lasting due to slow movement.

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.4 — AI BEHAVIOR & COMBAT LOGIC

Turtles are defensive tanks, not aggressors — unless cornered.

### Primary Behaviors

- bite snap
- shell swing
- body shove (knockback)
- shell tuck (damage reduction)

### Aggro Logic

Triggered by:

- stepping near nest or eggs
- attacking the turtle
- blocking its movement path

### Defense Logic

When below certain HP:

- Normal: shell tuck
- Rare: extended shell tuck with slow turn
- Elite: heavy shell brace (major reduction)
- Rare Elite: long-duration brace + counterbite pattern

### Flee Logic

Minimal:

- Normal: may retreat slightly
- Rare: often fights
- Elite: does NOT flee
- Rare Elite: NEVER flees

Turtles choose to survive, not run.

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.5 — WEATHER INTERACTION

### Rain

- marsh turtles roam wider
- rockbacks become slippery (reduced damage taken from slashing)

### Fog

- minimal impact
- easier to miss visually

### Snow

- turtles slow significantly
- tracks in snow become clear

### Storm

- turtles hunker down
- rarely move at all

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.6 — COMBAT PROFILE

### Damage

- low DPS
- high stagger on shove
- strong crushing damage from shell swing

### Defenses

- the highest armor of any family
- superb slashing resistance
- high resistance to bleed and poison
- nearly immune to basic arrows

### Weaknesses

- slow
- poor against fast predators
- soft underbelly (rare to hit)
- vulnerable when flipped (rare states in boss fights only)

# SECTION 30.7.8 — TAMER COMPANION SUMMARY

Turtles CAN be companions, but only:

- Normal
- Rare
- Elite

Rare Elite and Mini-Boss cannot be tamed.

### Companion Role: DEFENSE / TANK / BODYGUARD

### Strengths

- highest armor of all companions
- incredible damage soaking
- great for holding choke points
- intimidating presence
- very steady morale

### Weaknesses

- slow
- low DPS
- poor chase potential
- vulnerable in water (some species)

### Companion Behaviors

- Shell Brace
- Bite Snap
- Body Block
- Guard Circle
- Shell Slam (Elite only)

Turtles excel at protecting the player and holding flanks.

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.7.9 — FAMILY ROLE IN WORLD DESIGN

Turtles create:

- defensive encounters
- chokepoint challenges
- environmental storytelling (ancient shells)
- companion diversity (best tanks after bears)
- resource loops for armor crafting
- quiet pacing in swamps and forests

They embody the “endurance and patience” side of the ecosystem.
