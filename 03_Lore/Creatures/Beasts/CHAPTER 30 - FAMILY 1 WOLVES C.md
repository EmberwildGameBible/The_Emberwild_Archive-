# FAMILY 1 — WOLVES

“The forest breathes, and wolves are its pulse.”

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.1.0 — FAMILY IDENTITY

Wolves represent:

- pack coordination
- flanking intelligence
- instinctive hierarchy
- loyalty and morale dynamics
- cunning, efficient hunting

They are the benchmark predator in many regions and set the standard for how creatures behave in coordinated groups.

Wolves are not magical.

Their threat comes from:

- numbers
- tactics
- endurance
- territorial aggression

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.1.1 — BIOLOGY & ECOLOGY

### Habitat:

- Forests
- Plains
- Tundra
- Highland foothills
- Rare in deserts/swamps

### Diet:

- deer
- boars
- small mammals
- carrion

### Behavior Type:

- Pack Hunters (primary)
- Skittish Lone Wanderers (rare lone wolves)
- Territorial Predators (Heartlands)

### Season Influence:

- Winter: wolves roam more aggressively
- Summer: packs rest in shade, hunt at dusk/dawn
- Spring: dens active (very dangerous)
- Autumn: packs bulk up, large migrations possible

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.1.2 — TIER VARIANTS

All wolves follow your official tier structure:

### NORMAL WOLF

- Small to mid-sized wolf
- Standard health, moderate damage
- Pack of 2–4
- Predictable aggression range
- Common in Outer zones

### RARE WOLF

- unique coat (ashen, pale, striped)
- slightly higher stamina
- more cautious, slightly smarter flanks
- deeper footprint signs
- appears in both Outer + Inner zones

### ELITE WOLF

- pack leader
- old, scarred, heavier
- commands pack behavior
- improved health, damage, and turn speed
- higher morale, flees only when heavily wounded
- found mostly in Inner zones and Heartlands

### RARE ELITE WOLF

- a dominant, highly intelligent alpha
- scarred body, long stride, deep prints
- flanks aggressively
- rushes weak targets
- extremely rare
- signature drop: Alpha Fang / Pristine Pelt
- found only in Heartlands or deep Inner zones

### MINI-BOSS WOLF

- NOT tamable
- enormous rogue alpha
- exiled or mutated by natural hardship
- fights alone
- custom roar / intimidation sequence
- extremely high stamina and speed bursts
- handcrafted moveset (slam, bite, short lunge)
- always territorial in a fixed den high-danger area

### BOSS

- Wolf bosses only appear in dungeons (ruined keeps, caves).
- Unique attacks, environmental hazards, and lore connected to the region.

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.1.3 — TRACKING SIGNS

Wolf signs are some of the easiest to learn but hardest to master.

### Footprints

- narrow paws, deep pads
- Rare/Elite deeper, more spread
- Rare Elite stride length noticeably larger

### Scat

- common, used to mark territory
- fresher scat near den entrances

### Kill Sites

- partially eaten carcasses
- bite patterns readable by tracking skill

### Howls

- multi-direction echoes = pack present
- single directional call = lone wolf
- cascading howls = pack rallying

### Den Markings

- scratches at cave entrances
- strong scent presence
- bone piles (rare Elite / Mini-boss dens)

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.1.4 — BEHAVIOR PATTERNS (AI)

Wolves are the most tactically intelligent creatures in Emberwild.

### Pack Behavior

- circle and test player
- close gaps from multiple angles
- retreat if leader falls
- attempt coordinated lunges

### Aggro Logic

- engage when approached inside territory
- engage if player threatens pack member
- warn before attacking (growl, stance)

### Flee Logic

- Normal: flee at ~40% health
- Rare: flee at ~30%
- Elite: flee at ~20%
- Rare Elite: flee at ~15%
- Mini-Boss: rarely flees unless overwhelmed

### Preferred Targets

1. wounded players
2. player companions
3. isolated players
4. weakened creatures nearby (scavenging opportunity)

### Weather Influence

- fog: wolves become extremely dangerous
- rain: wolves roam wider
- snow: wolves conserve energy and hunt in tighter formation

────────────────────────

# SECTION 30.1.5 — COMBAT PROFILE

### Damage:

- bite (primary)
- claw rake (secondary)
- stagger lunge (elite and above)

### Defenses:

- high stamina
- good flanking resistance
- susceptible to bleed and piercing damage

### Weaknesses:

- predictable lunge telegraph
- morale breaks if pack is reduced
- fire and torches discourage approach

# SECTION 30.1.7 — TAMER COMPANION SUMMARY

Normal / Rare / Elite / Rare Elite wolves can be companions.

### Companion Role: FLANKER / DPS / TRACKING ASSIST

### Strengths:

- best flanking AI in the game
- high obedience potential
- great at chasing fleeing wildlife
- can “call out” nearby dangers
- high loyalty gain when bonded early

### Weaknesses:

- low armor
- vulnerable to boar charges
- low morale during heavy weather

### Wolf Companion Behaviors:

- Flank
- Track Assist
- Guard
- Harass
- Pack Stance (Elite and above: small buff when near other wolves)

Rare Elite wolves make exceptionally loyal companions but require long bonding.
