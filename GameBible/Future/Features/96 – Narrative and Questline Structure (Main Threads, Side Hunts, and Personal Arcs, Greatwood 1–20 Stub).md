# 96 – Narrative & Questline Structure: Main Threads, Side Hunts, and Personal Arcs (Greatwood 1–20 Stub)

## Purpose

Outline the **narrative scaffolding** for Greatwood 1–20:

- Main story threads
- Side quests & Hunts
- Personal arcs and character-focused lines
- How these plug into:
  - Lodge politics (83)
  - Corruption & Hollowroot (65, 91)
  - Professions and Ember Pot (52–59, 85)
  - World structure (63, 93, 94)

Goal: ensure quests feel like **cohesive storylines**, not random tasks, and create a template for expanding beyond 1–20.

---

## High-Level Narrative Pillars

1. **The Lodge & the Forest**
   - The player learns:
     - What the Lodge is.
     - What their responsibilities are.
     - The balance between hunting and stewardship.

2. **The Gathering Threat**
   - Corruption, Hollowroot, and ash-touched beasts (65, 91, 66).
   - Something is changing in Greatwood’s patterns.

3. **People of the Lodge**
   - Council members (83), profession leaders (59), and everyday hunters.
   - Personal histories and conflicts.

4. **The Season Arc**
   - A contained story of:
     - One “season” of increasing danger.
     - Culminating in a major confrontation and Ember Night reflection (94).

## Questline Categories

### 1. Main Story Threads

Core questlines that:

- Guide the player through:
  - Greatwood regions (63).
  - Hollowroot Warren introduction (65).
  - Lodge political beats (83).
  - Corruption baseline understanding (91).

Example main threads:

1. **“First Season” Thread**
   - Player’s introduction to:
     - Lodge.
     - Basic hunts.
     - Professions.
   - Ends with:
     - First major Deed success.
     - Lodge acknowledging the player as a proper hunter.

2. **“Hollow Beneath” Thread**
   - Reveals:
     - Hollowroot Warren.
     - Corrupted beasts.
   - Ends with:
     - First delve into Hollowroot.
     - Early-stage boss encounter.

3. **“Ash and Ember” Thread**
   - Focuses on:
     - Burn-Scars.
     - Ash-touched beasts.
     - Ember Pot contamination risk (56, 85).
   - Ends with:
     - Resolving or containing a specific ash-touched incident.
     - Possible changes to Ember Pot recipes and lore.

---

### 2. Side Quests & Hunts

Smaller arcs that:

- Are optional, but deepen:
  - Professions.
  - Outposts/settlements.
  - Individual NPCs.

Types:

1. **Profession Arcs**
   - Cook, Forester, Woodworker, Alchemy.
   - Each has:
     - Intro quests.
     - Mid-tier arcs.
     - Future mastery hooks.

2. **Regional Hunts & Stories**
   - Multi-step Hunts that:
     - Tie into local rumors and secrets (90).
   - Example:
     - “The One-Eyed Boar” arc:
       - Track rumors across different subregions.
       - Ends with a named Deed.

3. **Outpost Stories**
   - Each outpost or settlement (93) gets:
     - 2–3 side quest chains:
       - Supply issues.
       - Local disputes.
       - Threats encroaching.

---

### 3. Personal Arcs (Key NPCs)

Character-focused lines centered around:

- Council members.
- Profession leads.
- A few standout hunters or locals.

Examples:

1. **Keeper of the Ember – “Weight of the Pot”**
   - Deals with:
     - Food shortages.
     - Difficult choices about who gets fed.
   - Ties to:
     - Ember Pot state (85).
     - Ash contamination.
   - Culminates in:
     - Emotional scene at Ember Night Feast (94).

2. **Huntmaster – “Lines in the Trees”**
   - Explores:
     - Their past Deeds.
     - The cost of aggressive hunting philosophy.
   - Player:
     - Helps navigate a crisis where:
       - Over-hunting or underestimating a foe has consequences.

3. **Forester’s Circle Lead – “Roots and Boundaries”**
   - Focus:
     - How far to push into untouched Greatwood.
   - Ties into:
     - Corruption vs natural wildness tension (91).

Personal arcs:

- Provide:
  - Talent & profession unlock hooks.
  - Big lore dumps in a more human way.

## Structure & Flow (Greatwood 1–20)

### Act 1 – Arrival & Orientation

- Player arrives at Lodge or is newly inducted.
- Quests:
  - Basic Lodge tour.
  - First Hunts near Lodge.
  - Intro to Ember Pot, professions, and Hunt Board.
- Tone:
  - Curious, grounded, slightly ominous hints of future threats.

### Act 2 – Broadening the Hunt

- Player moves into:
  - Deeper Greatwood subregions (63).
  - Meets outposts/settlements (93).
- Quests:
  - Mid-tier Hunts.
  - Regional threats.
  - First signs of serious corruption and ash-touched beasts.
- Tone:
  - More danger, sense of increasing stakes.
  - Lodge politics surface.

### Act 3 – Deep Roots

- Focus:
  - Hollowroot Warren (65).
  - Burn-Scar crises.
  - Lodge under strain (food, morale, corruption).
- Quests:
  - Hollowroot delves.
  - Major Deeds and world bosses (66).
  - Personal arcs crest: council, profession leads.

### Act 4 – Ember Night

- Capstone:
  - Major conflict resolution (slayed/contained big threat).
  - Ember Night Feast and Remembrance (94).
- Outcomes:
  - Lodge survives, but:
    - New questions about the forest.
    - Hooks for future seasons and regions.
- Player:
  - Recognized as a core hunter of the Lodge.

## Quest Structure Details

### Branching Flavor vs Hard Branches

- Major choices:
  - Typically alter **flavor** and small outcomes:
    - Who approves/disapproves.
    - Which NPC’s view you support.
  - Avoid:
    - Hard content locks in 1–20.
- Examples:
  - Save more people vs secure more resources.
  - Take a more cautious vs aggressive path on a Deed.

### Recurring Quest Structures

- **Hunt Chains:**
  - 3–5 step arcs leading to a named Deed.
- **Investigation Chains:**
  - Follow clues about:
    - Missing hunters.
    - Strange tracks.
    - Silent grove.
- **Support Chains:**
  - Gradually help:
    - A settlement grow.
    - A profession wing solve a systemic issue.
