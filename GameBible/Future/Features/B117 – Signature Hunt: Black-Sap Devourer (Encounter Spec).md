# B117 – Signature Hunt: Black-Sap Devourer (Encounter Spec)
Purpose

Fully define the Black-Sap Devourer Signature Hunt encounter for the Greatwood 1–20 band:

Narrative framing and setup

Arena layout and environmental mechanics

Phase-by-phase combat flow

Integration with:

Corruption & Warding (91, B101, B109)

Damage types & statuses (B109)

Enemy families (B114)

Pockets & landmarks (B113)

Contract supports (B116 – SH-S2)

Roles & builds (B110)

Loot & Lodge reactions (B102, B104, B105, B106)

This is a boss encounter spec, not the quest chain wrapper (those are in B103, B115, B116).

Narrative Identity

What it is

A massive, corruption-swollen predator entrenched in the Black Sap Grove, acting as a mobile heart and mouth of the sap infection.

Not a demon or god, but an apex natural creature that has over-merged with Black Sap.

What it represents

The danger of letting corruption fester unchecked.

The tension between:

Warden control (contain and cauterize).

Scholar/Fringe curiosity (study, harness).

A test of the player’s ability to manage:

HCL.

Environmental hazards.

Sustained pressure.

Perception

Visually:

A hulking beast with half its body armor replaced by glossy resin plates.

Mouth and eyes leaking sap.

Tendrils rooting its limbs into the ground during some phases.

Auditory:

Wet, sucking sounds.

Deep, hollow bellows.

Sap “rain” dripping audibly from canopy.

Prerequisites & Entry
Recommended Level & State

Intended level band: 14–18.

Soft requirements:

At least one corruption-oriented contract completed (C1/C3 from B116).

Player has access to:

Basic Warden/anti-corruption gear (even if they do not use it).

A handful of mid-tier brews:

Cleanse, Purge, Warden’s Veil, at least one movement or mitigation brew.

Structural Prereqs

Quest/contract line:

Completion of SH-S2 – “The Sapward Test” (B116) is strongly recommended but not strictly mandatory.

If done:

Arena has ward-totem elements active, based on choices (Aelric vs Neris).

Some Warding Hall and Grove-related contracts completed:

To justify narrative access into the Devourer’s core thicket.

Hunt Setup

Start at Greatwood Lodge Hunt Board:

Signature Hunt listing:

“Black-Sap Devourer”

Briefing characters:

Aelric (Wardens) and Neris (Fringe Alchemist) both attend, with Maera as referee.

Pre-Hunt dialogue:

Aelric frames this as:

A required amputation of a diseased limb.

Neris frames it as:

A chance to retrieve irreplaceable sap data and shards.

Player is encouraged to:

Prepare corruption mitigation, movement tools, and fire/cleansing options.

Arena & Environment
Location

Deep within Black Sap Grove, at the Devourer’s Thicket (see B113).

Travel path:

Through mid-Grove.

Increasing ZCL from 2 up to 3–4 in the immediate lair area.

Base ZCL & Corruption Behavior

Lair base ZCL: 3

Always in effect while in arena.

During certain abilities/phases:

Local ZCL spikes to 4 in zones.

HCL:

Slowly increases over time while in arena.

Accelerates:

When standing in sap pools.

When hit by certain Devourer attacks.

Arena Layout

Rough layout (conceptual):

Circular / oval glade, ringed by twisted, sap-leaking trees.

Key elements:

Central Pit:

Shallow basin of semi-solid sap.

Devourer tends to anchor here in Phase 1 and 3.

Outer Ring:

More solid ground, though studded with:

Sap puddles.

Roots.

Sapfall edge:

One side of the arena has:

A mini Sapfall that continuously drips into a small pool.

Ward Totem Sites:

If SH-S2 was completed:

1–2 ward-totem pedestals present at specific points.

Environmental Hazards

Sap Pools (small and large)

Standing in:

Applies SLOW.

Accelerates HCL gain.

Some pools can:

Be burned off temporarily with fire/Alchemy.

Sapfall Zone

Standing under it:

Rapid HCL gain.

Occasional direct Corruption status burst.

Can be:

Partially blocked or mitigated by Warden wards in some variants.

Resin Spikes

Rise from ground in telegraphed patterns when Devourer uses certain abilities.

Deal:

Physical – Piercing damage.

May:

Impale and briefly Root if not avoided.

Ward Totems (if installed via SH-S2)

Aelric-configured:

Small safe zone:

Reduced ZCL.

Reduced HCL gain.

No SLOW from sap inside radius.

Neris-configured:

Volatile:

Periodically emit waves that:

Damage enemies and player.

Clear sap pools temporarily.

Player must avoid pulses or time them for advantage.

Encounter Structure

Three main phases plus soft intro and soft outro:

Phase 0 – Lair Approach & First Glimpse

Phase 1 – The Rooted Glutton

Phase 2 – The Sap Storm

Phase 3 – The Thrashing Heart

Post-Fight – Harvest & Moral Layer

Phase 0 – Lair Approach & First Glimpse
Objective

Ease player into:

Arena layout.

Sap mechanics.

Corruption pressure.

Flow

Small approach corridor:

Fight through:

2–3 packs of Sap-Wolves and Sap Leech Swarms.

One Resin Husk mini-elite as final gate.

At lair edge:

Short cutscene:

The Devourer is seen feeding on a massive resin dome or beast carcass.

Sap pulses around it, showing heavy corruption.

Player regains control:

Brief pre-fight window to:

Place temporary wards if any tools allow.

Drink brews.

Check environment.

Devourer then fully aggroes, moving into Phase 1.

Phase 1 – The Rooted Glutton

Fantasy: The Devourer is still partially rooted to its feeding pit, using long-range sap attacks and ground control more than mobility.

State

Devourer anchored mostly near center.

Movement:

Slow but constant turning.

Short lunges within central pit.

Adds:

Periodic small Sap Leech Swarms and maybe a Sap-Wolf or two.

Key Abilities

Sap Spit Volley

Telegraph:

Devourer rears head back, sap gathering in throat.

Effect:

Launches 3–5 mid-range sap projectiles in an arc.

On hit:

Moderate Physical + Corruption damage.

Applies CORRUPTION SICKNESS stacks.

Counterplay:

Dodge / block.

Use cover (resin mounds, trees).

Ground Seep

Telegraph:

Devourer slams forelimbs down.

Ground cracks faintly.

Effect:

Several small sap pools ooze up around target player positions after a short delay.

Status:

SLOW and HCL acceleration in pools.

Counterplay:

Move before ooze completes.

Fire/chemical brews can burn off specific pools temporarily.

Sap Whip

Telegraph:

A sap tendril pulls taut from body to ground.

Effect:

Mid-range sweep in 120–180 degree arc.

Damage:

Physical – Slashing.

Status:

Mild KNOCKBACK.

Counterplay:

Step out of arc.

Shield block for Warden types.

Call of the Leech

Periodic ability.

Summons:

Small Sap Leech Swarm.

Behavior:

Swarm targets backliners, adding corruption pressure if not cleared.

Player Learning Goals

Understand:

Sap pools and their consequences.

Basic corruption management in a boss fight.

Test:

Positioning.

Sustained mid-range combat.

When Devourer reaches ~70% HP, it transitions to Phase 2 via a brief, telegraphed roar and partial unrooting.

Phase 2 – The Sap Storm

Fantasy: The Devourer tears itself half-free and starts rampaging through the arena, drastically increasing chaos and Corruption output.

State

Movement:

Faster, more mobile.

Leaves sticky sap trails behind after certain moves.

Adds:

Fewer small adds.

Occasional Resin Husk emerges from the outer ring if not previously cleared in Phase 0/1.

New / Modified Abilities

Charge Through the Mire

Telegraph:

Devourer crouches low, sap streaming from legs.

Effect:

Fast linear charge toward target.

On path:

Leaves a thick sap trail:

Higher SLOW.

More HCL gain.

On impact:

Heavy Physical – Blunt, possible KNOCKDOWN.

Counterplay:

Sidestep.

Use movement brews.

Wardens can attempt to block for allies.

Sapstorm Bellow

Telegraph:

Devourer plants limbs, chest heaving.

Effect:

360-degree sap droplet storm:

Many small projectiles falling from above.

Damage:

Each droplet: chip damage + tiny Corruption.

Status:

If several hits land in a short window:

CORRUPTION SICKNESS.

Counterplay:

Move into partial cover.

Use shield or mitigation brews.

Aelric-style ward totems (if present) reduce damage inside radius.

Resin Spike Row

Telegraph:

Sap lines ripple along ground.

Effect:

A row or fan of spikes erupts from ground.

Damage:

Physical – Piercing.

Status:

Brief ROOT if directly impaled.

Counterplay:

Read ground indicators.

Move perpendicular to spike path.

Devouring Lunge

Telegraph:

Devourer rises back and focuses on a target.

Effect:

Long, high-arc leap to that target’s last position.

On landing:

Large impact zone:

Heavy damage.

Sap splash creating a large new pool.

Counterplay:

Watch lock-on behavior.

Roll out of marked impact area.

Wardens can intercept slightly with taunt-like effects if system supports it.

Ward Totem Integration (SH-S2)

Aelric Path:

One ward circle is active:

Reduced Corruption.

Reduced HCL gain.

Devourer occasionally:

Attempts to stomp/shatter it.

Player can:

Defend the totem.

Or choose to let it be destroyed (raising difficulty).

Neris Path:

Totem occasionally:

Builds up energy and releases:

Shockwave of Arcane/Fire damage.

Clears sap pools.

But:

Damages the player too if caught.

May cause the Devourer to change focus aggressively.

Player Learning Goals

Manage:

Moving hazards.

Burst windows.

Understand:

When to hold ground in safer zones.

When to risk crossing dangerous terrain.

At ~35–40% HP, Devourer enrages, collapsing partially into the central pit and triggering Phase 3.

Phase 3 – The Thrashing Heart

Fantasy: Cornered and enraged, the Devourer becomes more stationary again, but its corruption output spikes, turning the lair into a collapsing nightmare.

State

Devourer slumps into central pit:

Cannot easily leave.

Body partially fused to sap mass.

Movement:

Limited:

Rotations.

Short lunges.

Corruption:

Local ZCL near pit jumps to 4.

Adds:

Periodic, more significant waves:

Sap Leech Swarms.

Occasional Sapling Horror or mini Resin Husk.

New / Modified Abilities

Heart-Pulse Surge

Telegraph:

Devourer’s torso glows faintly; sap pulses in waves.

Effect:

Rapid pulses of Corruption aura emanate from center.

Damage/Status:

Corruption damage + fast HCL gain near center.

Counterplay:

Approach only in short bursts.

Retreat to outer ring between pulses.

Use Warden’s Veil and cleanse brews.

Sap Geyser

Telegraph:

Several points around arena begin bubbling.

Effect:

After short delay, vertical sap jets erupt.

Damage:

High on direct hit.

Status:

SLOW + heavy Corruption Sickness.

Counterplay:

Keep moving.

Note bubbling points ahead of time.

Devourer’s Grasp

Telegraph:

Sap tendrils spread from central pit.

Effect:

Attempts to grab and yank a player toward central pit.

Status:

ROOT when grabbed, then slow pull.

Counterplay:

Break free via:

Heavy attack.

Ally interrupt.

Specific brews or Ward abilities.

Last Frenzy

When HP < 15%.

Devourer chains:

Sap Geyser.

Sapstorm Bellow.

Very dangerous window:

Player must:

Time big defensive tools.

Use execute windows smartly.

Victory Condition

Devourer HP reaches zero.

Final animation:

Body slumps.

Sap shell cracks.

Black sap gushes and recedes slowly into ground.

ZCL in immediate area:

Drops from 3–4 to 1–2 in the aftermath, for some time.

Encounter Difficulty & Role Spotlights
Huntmaster Striker

Strength:

High damage windows between large telegraphed attacks.

Challenges:

Must weave in and out of:

Sap trails.

Sapstorm.

Needs:

Strong self-sustain or team support for Corruption.

Warden Frontliner

Strength:

Soaking:

Sap spit lines.

Charges.

Some spike rows for team.

Using:

Ward gear and Warden’s Veil to stabilize ground.

Challenges:

If they over-commit near center in Phase 3:

HCL can spiral.

Scholar Field Controller

Strength:

Clearing:

Sap Leech Swarms.

Adds.

Pre-clearing:

Spore-like hazards (if any) and controlling lanes.

Challenges:

Must not overstay in heavy ZCL zones.

Needs to juggle:

Offense.

Control.

Cleansing support roles.

Corruption-Edge Hunter

Strength:

Access to:

Corruption-scaling brews.

Can:

Turn moderate HCL into damage gains.

Challenges:

This fight can easily push them into:

Dangerous HCL territory.

Requires real discipline in when to cleanse.

Versatile Woodsman

Strength:

Good all-round toolkit:

Movement.

Enough cleansing.

Enough damage.

Leveraging:

Preparedness with food, brews, wards.

Challenges:

Not the best at any one survival angle:

Must play smart and exploit knowledge.

Corruption & HCL Tuning

Baseline:

Minor HCL gain passively in arena.

Standing in sap:

Additional HCL per second.

Getting hit by Corruption-tagged attacks:

Burst HCL chunks.

Tools:

Warden’s Veil, Purge of the Glade, Infirmary services after fight:

Recommended to manage long-term HCL.

Fail conditions (non-numeric, design intent):

Player should feel:

They can win the fight even with some HCL spikes.

But:

Going in repeatedly without cleansing:

Leads to increased mental/corruption side effects in later Greatwood content.
